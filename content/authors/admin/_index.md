Adel Bibi is a Senior Researcher in Machine Learning and Computer Vision at the <a href="https://eng.ox.ac.uk/people/adel-bibi/">Department of Engineering Science</a> at the <a href="https://www.ox.ac.uk/">University of Oxford</a>, a Research Member of the Common Room and a former Junior Fellow at <a href="https://www.kellogg.ox.ac.uk/our-people/adel-bibi/">Kellogg College</a>, and a member of the <a href="https://ellis.eu/">ELLIS Society</a>. He is also an R&D Distinguished Advisor at <a href="https://www.softserveinc.com/en-us">SoftServe</a>. Previously, he was a Senior Research Associate and postdoctoral researcher in the Torr Vision Group at Oxford working with <a href="https://www.robots.ox.ac.uk/~phst/">Philip H.S. Torr</a>. He obtained his MSc and PhD from KAUST in 2016 and 2020, respectively, under the supervision of <a href="http://bernardghanem.com/">Bernard Ghanem</a>.

His research focuses on trustworthy and robust AI, agentic AI safety, and adversarial machine learning. He received the <strong>Horizon Europe Grant</strong> in 2026, the <strong>Coefficient Giving Grant</strong> in 2026, the <a href="https://www.aisi.gov.uk/work/strengthening-ai-resilience"><strong>Systemic AI Safety Grant</strong></a> from the UK AI Security Institute in 2025, the <strong>Toyota Motor Europe Grant</strong> in 2025, the <strong>Google Gemma 2 Academic Award</strong> in 2024, and an <a href="https://www.amazon.science/research-awards/program-updates/fall-2021-and-winter-2022-amazon-research-awards-recipients-announced"><strong>Amazon Research Award</strong></a> in 2022. His recent work on vulnerabilities in AI agents, transferable adversarial attacks, and AI safety evaluations has received broad international attention, including coverage by <a href="https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/hacking-ai-agents-how-malicious-images-and-pixel-manipulation-threaten"><em>Scientific American</em></a>, <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2025/nov/04/experts-find-flaws-hundreds-tests-check-ai-safety-effectiveness"><em>The Guardian</em></a>, <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/tech-news/ai-chatgpt-test-smart-capabilities-may-exaggerated-flawed-study-rcna241969"><em>NBC News</em></a>, <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDUC-LqVrPU"><em>Computerphile</em></a>, <a href="https://actions.tldrnewsletter.com/web-version?ep=1&lc=e818ae92-999d-11ee-a2f2-01fa339a07aa&p=5470c1da-c59d-11ee-85a6-d92706d36a5f&pt=campaign&t=1707315962&s=4586e6a094085955c0463173be84d5e1e6626da9a069ea66ea8035baa6610d62"><em>TLDR News</em></a>, <a href="https://globoplay.globo.com/v/14064103/"><em>Globo TV</em></a>, and the <a href="https://huggingface.co/spaces/OpenEvals/evaluation-guidebook#how-tokenization-can-mess-up-your-evaluation"><em>Hugging Face Evaluation Guidebook</em></a>. His work has received multiple paper and service distinctions, including <span><strong>four best/outstanding workshop paper awards</strong></span> (<a href="https://www.adelbibi.com/post/prefix_tuning_bes_paper/">NeurIPS'23</a>, <a href="/post/pinss_paper/">ICML'23</a>, <a href="/post/best_extended_abstract_cvpr22/">CVPR'22</a>, <a href="/post/big_data_optimization_kaust/">OBD'18</a>), <span><strong>four outstanding reviewer awards</strong></span> (CVPR18, CVPR19, ICCV19, ICLR22), and a <a href="/post/notable_ac_neurips23/"><strong>Notable Area Chair Award at NeurIPS 2023</strong></a>. He regularly serves as a Senior Area Chair and Area Chair for major AI conferences including NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, etc. Bibi has authored more than 50 publications in top-tier machine learning and computer vision venues, including NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, CVPR, ICCV, ECCV, AAAI, and TPAMI.
